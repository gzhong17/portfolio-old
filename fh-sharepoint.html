Fraser Health SharePoint

SUMMARY OF APPLICATION

The SharePoint Workspaces home page is the main “hub” that introduces SharePoint and the services that Fraser Health provides in regards to SharePoint. The page directs users to fill in request/help forms, sign up for training, directs users to our Workspaces support site, and post of upcoming events.

The SharePoint Workspaces Support Site houses a library of support articles. Help articles are divided by introduction, user basics, and administrator levels. Fraser Health users can follow through these tutorials of how to use SharePoint.

Final Design

MY ROLE

During this project, I had taken up the responsibilities of the web graphic design work completed on the SharePoint home page and support page within Fraser Health’s intranet site.

My tasks included analyzing the current design, identify user requirements, research, create mock ups, design icons, and build the final implementation.

DESIGN PROCESS

Identify problems ⇢ Identify key requirements ⇢ Research ⇢ Sketch layouts and graphics ⇢ Build mockups via Illustrator and Photoshop ⇢ Implement in SharePoint environment

User concerns and requirements were identified through conducting interviews with Fraser Health employees and supervisors.

PROBLEMS IDENTIFIED

Home Page

  • Page was blocky
  • Information wasn’t presented effectively (information was buried)
  • Icons were non­descriptive

Support Page

  • Page was extremely long
  • Too much information on a single page

RESEARCH

  • Observe Microsoft, Skype, Pinterest sites
  • Noticed that often there are quick links/ categories that divide up topics to prevent overloading information
  • Important topics were placed near the top
  • FAQ was often used to help users easily find answers to common questions or popular topics
  • Use of graphics helps soften and refresh appearance

KEY REQUIREMENTS

  • Important information should be clearly visible
  • Shorter page to avoid burying information
  • Appearance could be refreshed (eg: using photos to bring warmth)
  • Icons could be redesigned to reflect what it represents

CHALLENGES & DECISIONS

Some challenges I met include finding the most suitable way to organize the page, identifying which elements were important (or not), and be graphically creative but look professional for a corporate setting. I made different variations of icons and colour schemes, which was reviewed and approved by my supervisors.

I had realized the big blocky headers on the original page were unhelpful and pushed information towards the bottom of the page. A cleaner appearance, and regrouping information into a simple, concise section was decided upon this observation.

The support page needed restructuring as the original page was overwhelming for users due to the endless list of articles on the page. A decision was made to move user basics and administrator topics to separate pages and instead have a simple link on the main support page that points to the two separate pages.

In order to guide users to the appropriate page, I created banners that clearly label sections respective to the level of the user. Furthermore, by providing a short description with the names of most commonly read articles, users can easily familiarize with these structural changes through these cues.

Along with using graphics, colour, fonts, proximity, and other design principles, I made sure users can visually understand the changes made in the new design and easily navigate within.

OUTCOME & GROWTH

Being conscious of how information is presented affects users’ experiences with the product or application. Users like efficiency and as designers, it is our job to make their process as smooth and quick as possible.

If we are not careful with choosing what information we share and the way it is shared, users often will be unable to find what they are looking for and become frustrated.
