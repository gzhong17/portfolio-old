SFU Beedie

SUMMARY OF APPLICATION

The Beedie Social Media Hub was developed for housing all of SFU Beedie's social media channels and recent news.

Here you can find the latest event photos and new posts from Beedie's Wordpress blogs. Also never miss another twitter update!

MY ROLE

This was a solo project where I had to do user research, design layout, and figure out the structure of where the live feeds would be coming from.

Originally, SFU Beedie's social media channels were posted separately on their website. In an effort to traffic to the channels and improve convenience for the students, this social media hub was built.

DESIGN PROCESS

Identify key requirements ⇢ Research existing examples ⇢ Sketch layouts and graphics ⇢ Build components using HTML, CSS, PHP, jQuery, and API

KEY REQUIREMENTS

Interviews were conducted with my supervisor and other staff from Beedie Marketing and Communications department.

  • Consolidate all SFU Beedie social media channels
  • Ensure easy access and browsing
  • Feeds and widgets should not break
  • Picture and blog feeds must update accordingly

Sketches and Implementation

CHALLENGES & DECISIONS

A major challenge I had with this project was learning new programming languages and tools such as PHP, jQuery, and API. I was unfamiliar with them and had to learn them on the job. Through personal research on these topics and learning from my supervisor, I was able to complete the project.

Another obstacle I came across was figuring out how to display all the social media channels on the page while keeping them easily accessible and not hidden. The solution I came up with was to build a tab that can toggle different social media platforms, and then show the appropriate set of accounts for the chosen platform.

Through the use of icons, fonts, lines, and colour, these subtle cues help users navigate across the different sections of the page. The grey highlight when hovering or selecting different channels of social media also indicates which accounts are selected as well as visually directing the user towards the list of accounts because of the arrow shape. The icons also help provide recognition and label the source of the posts or photos (eg: Flickr, Twitter).

OUTCOME & GROWTH

My growth as a designer and developer has drastically grown since I tackled this project. A lesson I took away from this assignment was to keep trying new solutions out instead of hesitating and become overwhelmed with the unknown.

Also, I learned to always consider what content is most important to the user and give appropriate cues to help the user navigate to it.
